## Purpose

Define the durable business model assumptions for Open Video Studio.

## Business Value

AI video products can have significant compute, storage, bandwidth, and model costs. A clear business model keeps product, pricing, infrastructure, and customer value aligned.

## Problem Statement

Traditional SaaS pricing does not always map cleanly to AI media workloads. Unlimited usage can destroy margins, while overly restrictive credit systems can damage adoption and trust.

## Scope

Likely monetization pillars:

- Subscription plans based on seats, collaboration, governance, storage, and workflow depth.
- Usage-based credits or metered consumption for AI generation, rendering, transcription, exports, and heavy automation.
- Team and enterprise plans for permissions, brand controls, admin, security, compliance, and support.
- Add-ons for high-volume generation, premium models, advanced automations, dedicated infrastructure, or enterprise integrations.
- API pricing for developer and operational use cases.

Business model principles:

- Price according to customer value and platform cost.
- Make usage understandable before users consume expensive resources.
- Avoid surprise billing.
- Preserve a path from individual creator to team and enterprise expansion.
- Treat credits, limits, and entitlements as product experience, not only billing mechanics.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Future pricing specs can reference these monetization pillars.
- Credit or usage systems must define user experience, cost controls, and transparency.
- Infrastructure decisions can consider margin implications.

## Future Evolution

Detailed packaging, credit logic, discounts, procurement, and enterprise terms should be defined in dedicated PRDs and billing architecture documents.

## AI Context

Use this document for business model direction. Do not invent exact prices, credit rates, or billing logic without a dedicated product requirement document.
