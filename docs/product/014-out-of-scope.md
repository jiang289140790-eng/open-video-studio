## Purpose

Define what Open Video Studio should intentionally avoid or delay.

## Business Value

Clear exclusions protect focus, reduce waste, and keep the platform from absorbing every video-adjacent idea.

## Problem Statement

AI video is a broad and fast-moving category. Without explicit exclusions, roadmap pressure can pull the product toward games, social networks, consumer entertainment, generic file storage, or model demos that do not strengthen the core platform.

## Scope

Out of scope unless explicitly reapproved:

- Building a consumer social network.
- Competing primarily as a stock media marketplace.
- Becoming a general-purpose cloud drive.
- Building a professional desktop non-linear editor as the primary product.
- Chasing model demos that do not support production workflows.
- Supporting every publishing platform before core creation and export workflows are reliable.
- Fully autonomous publishing without review, controls, and safety systems.
- Enterprise compliance claims before security and operational foundations exist.
- Exact pricing, credit rates, or entitlement rules without dedicated billing documentation.

Delayed until foundations exist:

- Marketplace ecosystem.
- Advanced developer platform.
- Large-scale autonomous campaign generation.
- Deep enterprise governance.
- Performance optimization loops based on external distribution data.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Product proposals can be rejected or delayed using explicit scope reasoning.
- Roadmap decisions can distinguish not-now from never.
- Feature map discussions in `PB-010` remain focused.

## Future Evolution

Items may move into scope through approved product specs and architecture decisions. Moving an item into scope should update this document and related roadmap docs.

## AI Context

Use this document to prevent scope creep. If a user asks for an excluded item, explain the conflict and suggest the closest in-scope alternative.
