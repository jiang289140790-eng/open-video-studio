## Purpose

Define motion and animation principles for Open Video Studio.

## Requirements

- Use motion to explain state, continuity, feedback, progress, or attention.
- Respect reduced-motion needs.
- Avoid decorative motion in work surfaces.

## Scope

Motion should explain continuity, state, causality, and progress. It should never distract from creative review, editing precision, or operational clarity.

Motion roles:

- Transition: moving between views, panels, or states.
- Feedback: confirming action, save, selection, or completion.
- Progress: generation, rendering, upload, export, or automation state.
- Attention: warning, failure, conflict, or required decision.
- Spatial continuity: opening panels, expanding timelines, revealing inspectors.

Usage rules:

- Motion should be subtle, fast, and purposeful.
- Long-running AI or render operations need clear progress communication.
- Avoid decorative looping motion in work surfaces.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Never hide important state changes behind animation alone.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Motion communicates state or continuity.
- Users can understand progress and interruption states.
- Reduced-motion alternatives exist for essential interactions.
- Animation does not compromise editing precision or accessibility.

## Future Evolution

Future token work may define motion durations, easing categories, and interaction patterns. Implementation details belong outside this document.

## Future Plan

Define motion token categories and examples for panels, generation progress, rendering, review, and command surfaces.

## AI Context

Use motion to clarify cause and effect. Do not recommend animations for delight unless they support comprehension or confidence.
