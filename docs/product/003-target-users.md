## Purpose

Define the durable target user segments for Open Video Studio.

## Business Value

Clear user segmentation prevents generic product design, improves roadmap sequencing, guides pricing, and helps engineering understand workflow complexity.

## Problem Statement

"People who make videos" is too broad. A solo creator, a growth marketer, an agency producer, and an enterprise brand team have different collaboration, quality, governance, and speed requirements.

## Scope

Primary target users:

- Content creators who need faster ideation, editing, repurposing, and publishing preparation.
- Marketing teams that produce campaigns, ads, social clips, product videos, and lifecycle content.
- Agencies and studios that need repeatable client workflows, versioning, approvals, and brand control.
- SaaS and ecommerce teams that need product demos, tutorials, launches, and performance creative.

Secondary target users:

- Educators and course creators.
- Internal enablement and support teams.
- Media operators managing high-volume content adaptation.

Key buyer and user roles:

- Creator: produces and edits content.
- Marketer: defines campaign goals and performance requirements.
- Producer: coordinates workflow, review, and delivery.
- Brand owner: enforces quality and consistency.
- Admin: manages workspace, billing, permissions, and governance.
- Developer or automation operator: integrates workflows through APIs or automations.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Feature specs identify which target user and role they serve.
- Roadmap items can explain whether they serve primary or secondary users.
- Pricing and permissions can map to buyer and operator roles.

## Future Evolution

Segments may be subdivided by company size, industry, workflow maturity, and compliance requirements. Enterprise user modeling should reference future security and governance documents.
